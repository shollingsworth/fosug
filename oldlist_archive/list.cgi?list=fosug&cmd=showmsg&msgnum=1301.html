You know that OpenOffice.org Impress can export your presentations or 
power points as a flash movie (.swf file).
Also, you can use VNCRec to record your presentation directly from your 
desktop.  (VNCRec http://www.sodan.org/~penny/vncrec).  I also use 
vnc2swf at work to create training presentations.
(vnc2swf http://www.unixuser.org/~euske/vnc2swf/)

With vnc2swf, you can import audio into it as well with command line 
tools.  Using Audacity you can capture the audio of the presentation and 
later dub it into the flash video.

Here's a good write up http://linuxgazette.net/102/washko.html
