Ronnie,

There are four methods I use and more exist but they're derivatives of the
four below.  I ordered them in preference order.  Nvu is part of the
distribution and will install from Ubuntu on the web.  The nvu site probably
had a .deb (Debian package) which would also work but not needed in this
case unless you can't figure out Way 1 on Kbuntu.

Way 1. May be different for Kbuntu but in Ubuntu select Applications -
Add/Remove - enter nvu in search field (its part of the distribution)

Way 2. Download a .deb of the application you want and click it

Way 3. covert an RPM with alien (can be iffy)

Way 4. compile tarball (a whole other subject best left alone for now)
