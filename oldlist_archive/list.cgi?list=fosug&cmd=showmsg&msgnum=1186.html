> Does the Powerchute software run on Linux? And does the USB connector

Yes it does. It's cross platform. Runs on pretty much all of the UNIX and
Unix-like platforms out there. And can be cross-platform. I.e. monitor UNIX
hosts from Windows clients. They have a couple different versions. I used the
old version that wasn't cross-platform.

I used to hear really good things about NUTS http://www.networkupstools.org/.
You can monitor on one system, with several connected to the UPS and shut all
of them down.

I have no idea on the USB setups. I would assume that APCs software would work
find with it. Note, that it's not FOSS, if you're pedantic in your software
usage. My UPSes are all serial. (I believe they actually pre-date USB
connections on UPSes, or were just starting to show up.)

And I was actually way wrong on the power factor. It's actually .6. 

> 
> On a side note, I wonder why desktop/server power supplies don't just
> come with battery backups in them? The computer runs internally on DC
> (9V?), the batteries provide DC. This all seems to work fine on laptops
> (when the batteries work, that is). A UPS has to go through the extra
> conversion from the battery DC power to AC output. (Only to be converted
> back by the computer's own power supply).

PCs are 12V, 5V and 3.3V. So it is possible. The power supply would need to be
MUCH larger than they currently are. Or the battery would need to be
piggy-backed. 

The closest I've seen to this is some mini-ITX cases have a 12V power supply
for running off of say a car (for use as Nav, DVD, MP3, etc.) There was a brick
type external power supply, like a laptop, ink jet, etc.
