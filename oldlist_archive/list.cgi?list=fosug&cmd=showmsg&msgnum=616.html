--- "Ian R. Justman" ####@####.#### wrote:

> 
> Hi, all.
> 
> Just installed one machine, Kurumi, and another
> which was actually a 
> reinstall, Yuushi, with Ubuntu Breezy.  Both are
> multimedia boxen, 
> specifically, anime screening boxes.
> 
> Everything went very smoothly once I realized I had
> to enable universe 
> and multiverse to get things working the way I
> needed.  Adding 
> ftp.nerim.net and I was REALLY good to go (so I can
> play DVDs and 
> anything requiring a Windows DLL to work).  Also
> added Kubuntu because I 
> prefer KDE over GNOME.
> 
> The one thing that struck me is that IEEE 1394 and
> USB 2.0 storage 
> devices worked with minimal fuss.  In fact, the
> bonus I gained was that 
> I don't have to worry about mounting the device I
> plug in; it appears on 
> my desktop.  Adding files from those devices is also
> a snap since any 
> devices plugged in have their own "folder" in the
> standard KDE dialog.
> 
> The only thing I don't like is Ubuntu's heavy
> reliance on sudo.  I 
> prefer an actual root prompt personally.  And since
> I have further 
> issues with sudo, I'm not so sure I want Ubuntu
> running as a server OS. 
>   However, for a desktop and a non-mission-critical
> infrastructure 
> machine, it's wonderful.
> 
> Overall, Ubuntu looks like a great desktop OS.  For
> multimedia, once you 
> enable and add the appropriate repositories, it's as
> good as or better 
> than Debian.  Further, the KDE build that you can
> get is rock-solid, 
> though Kaffeine, my player of choice, has a few
> bugs, but that seems to 
> be more an upstream problem than anything else.  It
> has issues in Debian 
> as well.
> 
> Those're my experiences.  Hope this helps people
> along!
> 
> --Ian.
