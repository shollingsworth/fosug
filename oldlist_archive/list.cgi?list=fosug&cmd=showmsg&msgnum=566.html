Hello, 


This message pertains about MTA administration. I could mark it with
a keyword, but there it is, anyway.

In summary: If your site does SMTP service, and must be connected-to, 
from anyone who would be using (connecting through) SBC, you will
probably want to support "Port 587" connections, in your site's SMTP
server -- like, very soon -- or they will not be able to connect.


In exposition:


Today, I received a message by way of an old email account with
SBC. Here was the gist -- what caught my attention, there:

  Because of the Port 25 filters, Members using a private company,
  university or hosting provider mail server (i.e. non-SBC Yahoo! mail
  server) ** will not be able to send outgoing email from dynamic
  DSL and Dialup connections ** without taking the steps outlined
  by SBCIS at: http://help.sbcglobal.net/article.php?item=4640

(** Emphasis added **)


The "steps", outlined there, do amount to little more than some very
short suggestions. 


(I might pity a user who would have to address such to an
administator, while not understanding the stustance of it. SBC's
writers do not do well in describing any substantive qualities of it,
either)

To the point, probably the primary one to address, here:

SBC mentions a port 587 and an RFC 2476 -- something about SMTP --
seeeming like the most viable thing for resolving SBC's new port 25
"filtering". 

One could resolve it, in other means, if one has the facilities for a
VPN, but "port 587" support  should  be the most feasible.


To my question:

I do not know what port 587 is about, or RFC 2476, such that it would 
be addressed with Postfix, Exim, Qmail, Sendmail, Courier, or
however. If anyone would have any words to mention about such,
esp. about Postfix --- of manual pages, config directives, or however
-- I would greatly appreciate heraing it.

I've started digging around, about it,  for postfix. 

(I intend to bookmark something about this, in case it would become a
more compelling issue, later. I thought it should be addressed to the
list, furthermore)


In short: I wanted to present this to the list -- in case it would be
of concern -- and with some hope that anyone  might know more, of what
to do about it, for supporting "port 587" in any of the common email
servers. 



Here's an HTML version of the RFC 2476, which was mentioned in the
stated page at SBC:
 http://rfc.sunsite.dk/rfc/rfc2476.html
