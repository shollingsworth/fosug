Note, to make a flash or jump, or thumb drive usable in linux. You
need 3 things compiled in your kernel. Either monolithic (all in 1
kernel) or in moduals.

USB Support
USB Mass storage
and
(whatever Filesystem your card is) In 99% of cases it's Fat16 (so you
would use vfat)

Once thoes are compiled and running, simply plug in device.  if you
check the dmesg log you will see it showup as a Scsi drive.  /dev/sda
for example. then you mount it like any normal drive

mount /dev/sda1 /mnt/flash
or whatever they happen to be.


Second note. ALL USB Flash/Pen/Thumb/Jump/Toe drives are linux
compatable (unless they are a crappy knock off, and I havn't seen a
non-standard knock off in about 7 years).  Easiest way to verify if
you will have problems with your drive.  PLug it into any windows ME
or XP computer and if It detects without the need for drivers, it is a
standard MASS STORAGE USB device.  Virtualy every usb storage device
uses this, harddrives, flash, most digital cameras. etc
