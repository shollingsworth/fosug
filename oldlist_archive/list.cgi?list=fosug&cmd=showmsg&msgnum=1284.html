Greetings tech gurus,

I'm nothing but a lurker on this list (as I am practically a luddite  
compared to you all), but I experienced something funky with the  
City's new Wifi network and I was hoping y'all could shed a little  
light on it.

If you haven't heard, the City is offering free wifi on the Fulton  
Mall between Fresno and Tulare Streets and also on Kern Street (you  
can see the transmitter thingies on lightposts and on the Mall's  
clock tower). So last night I went down there to try it out.

I joined the "cityoffresnofreewifiproject" network and got on. It  
took me to a start page with the city's logo on it, and I clicked a  
button that said "connect".  Tried to get to my blog to post, but  a  
series of two pop up windows opened saying that the certificates were  
not verified and did I want to proceed anyway (seemed like they were  
messages from Lok Technology, which I think is running security for  
the WiFi network?)  I honestly paid no attention to these messages  
and am not really sure what they said. I just clicked "ok" to get on  
my merry way. Then I connected to fresnofamous.com, but when I tried  
to post a new blog entry, I got this message:

Anomalous Behavior Detected -- Request Blocked

Your computer was automatically blacklisted (blocked) by the network  
due to an abnormal amount of activity originating from your  
connection. It is possible that your computer is infected with a worm  
and/or virus. Please contact your network administrator for  
assistance or more information.

I'm running a powerbook G4 with OS X 10.4.4 and Firefox 1.5.0.4.  
Pretty confidant I don't have a virus. On the WiFi start page these  
companies were listed: Mystic Technologies Inc, Unwired Broadband,  
Lok Technology, and Proxim Wireless Networks.

When I got home to my DSL line, I cleared my cache and was good to go  
again. Can anyone tell me what this means? Is the City's free WiFi  
out to get me?!

Thanks for listening!!
