* I use a bayesian filter that classifies spams probability out of 100%.  For
* messages to be classified as HAM, they must be rated <= 5% for them to be
* classified as SPAM, they must rate >= 95%.  Everything in the middle is
* rated as unsure and is put in a location that I then go through the messages
* and classify them by hand as ham/spam.
* For those wondering that this may be a painful process, please note that I
* get ~100 mails/day of which about 60 are spam.  I just sat down to process
* my spam-close folder (the unsure items) and have 66 messages over the last
* 10 days!  This includes ~20 virus mails and ~15 emails that are legitimate
* mails that had questionable content (or are from new people).  The rest are
* actual spams.
