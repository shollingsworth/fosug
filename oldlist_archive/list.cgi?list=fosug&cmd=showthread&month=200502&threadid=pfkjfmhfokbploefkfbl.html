Bzzzzz!  Care to guess again?  The only way to stop spam is to make it
non-profitable.  Trusted user lists are the same solution as RBLs with a shift
in the implementation point.  Is your friend a friend of Bills?  That's the
current Redmond point of view.  Fun thing there is who gets to control the
list.

> Honestly this is the way I see things going. These bastards,
> bastardize smtp and what is really to be expected from such a plain
> text weak protocol? Give me a break.

The protocol isn't to blame, per se.  It was invented back when people were
trusting (a la telnet).  The protocol is actually quite robust.  The fact that
it is being used for evil doesn't demonize the protocol.  I'm not certain that
there's a protocol solution for email anyway.  All of the suggestions I've
heard to date only use the protocol as the next step in the spam arms race
(while simultaneously breaking email for all who don't adopt).

> As an admin I'm tired of dealing with this BS. It's frustrating...

I'm a fan of caffeine and woodworking.

> It's enough to turn any of us sour.

Maybe.  At this point, I'm fairly well amused with a majority of the spam that
I see.  Some of it is rank, but for the most part, the ludicrous nature of the
bulk of the spam is hilarious.

One of the things that would help immensely would be to rid the net of html
email, en mass.  If it has a content-type text/html header in it, it doesn't
make it to the destination.  That takes care of microspams and the bulk of the
'tricks' in use by spammers today.
