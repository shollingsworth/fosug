You know, I tried out gentoo last year (or year before last.  Can't
remember) and while the speed for this OS was the best in Linux that I have
seen, the user experience during the install was the hardest of any linux
install in recent memory.  I guess the current installers in other distros
are making me lazy.

I have to really give the Gentoo crowd credit however.  That have taken 95
percent of that pain away.  The liveCD installer made everything point and
click (including compile options).  My only remaining problems came after
install when I realized that selecting xsession and xdm were not the right
choices for a display manager and login screen.  Luckily I had a laptop here
and was able to find the way you change that via command line and nano.

If you are a longtime Gentoo User and have not used the livecd install, I
recommend you boot off of the cd and check it out.  Its pretty nice.

My only remaining issues are to set the graphical bootup to a higher
resolution (the section where devices are loading is currently at 800x600 or
640x480.  This on a 19 inch monitor)  and then to figure out how to add
mozilla and other new applications into grub's menu after emerging them in.
