Hi, all.

Just a heads-up, it appears that SORBS has listed multiple netblocks 
within Time Warner Telecom's addresses as being "dynamic", even though 
they are, in fact, not.

Apparently, TWTelecom has to have any such blocks removed from SORBS.  I 
had to put in a couple of whitelists in our servers at work.

Also, doesn't anyone know of any DNSBL which isn't so militantly-run as 
SORBS is?  Honestly, I'd love to not to have to use it to begin with... 
  Ah well, here's to wishful thinking~!

I've been dealing with the same thing. What's saved me has been the fact
I have a little whitelisting script that puts entries both in the ACCESS
and amavis services so it bypasses certain domains and addresses.

Here's my list of RBL entries.

reject_rbl_client bl.spamcop.net
reject_rbl_client blackholes.easynet.nl
reject_rbl_client list.dsbl.org
reject_rbl_client rbl-plus.mail-abuse.org
reject_rbl_client relays.ordb.org
reject_rbl_client sbl.spamhaus.org
reject_rbl_client dnsbl.sorbs.net
reject_rbl_client cbl.abuseat.org
reject_rbl_client dnsbl.njabl.org

You guys remember when that one RBL blocked EVERYTHING!

I think that's why most people don't like using them so much is because
you hand over a lot of control of your mail services.
