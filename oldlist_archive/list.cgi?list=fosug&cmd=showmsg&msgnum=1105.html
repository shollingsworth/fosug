Thanks, I'll have to check those out.

Also, there's another platform I want to try out mainly to say I have, 
and that's the RouterBoard 500 series (http://www.routerboard.com) 
Those look pretty sweet and they have much better ability to upgrade 
storage.  I didn't look very closely on the RAM side of things, however. 
  Though at least with these, you could put multiple radios into the 
device, doing the multiple-SSID thing that way.

> The more I think about this, multiple SSID support is just going to be
> implemented via subinterfaces on the wireless interface.  This is non-trivial
> but I'm confident that you can figure it out and then give a talk on it at a
> meeting. :-)

I wouldn't be surprised that this is a non-trivial thing to do, 
considering that consumer radios have just one radio, and thus one MAC 
address.  And essentially, that's what they're doing, including 
implementing discrete security models per device (e.g. WPA on one, WEP 
on another, and 802.1x on yet another), as well as AP isolation, but I 
wanted to take it an additional step, securing my wired network from the 
additional subnet I'd be setting up.
