On 2006.04.25 23:33:46 -0700, Ian R. Justman wrote:
> Dennis Aston wrote:
> >Maybe this is why more router firmwares are not GPL'd.  With all of the
> >added features, I feel 0 need to upgrade to a newer router.  I will have to
> >seriously think twice as I won't be able to flash the newer ones 
> >apparently.
> 
> Though the DD-WRT project does have a "micro" image that'll fit into a 
> 2-megabyte flash on the v5 WRT54G, though I hear that takes some doing.

I initially installed the DD-WRT project on my router and had no end of
problems.  I'm fairly certain that these were inherited from the sveasoft core
as the same problems were attributed to the sveasoft releases.  The problem:
my router would stay up for a couple of days and then simply refuse to
associate clients.  Since I have it connected to a wall plug that is on a
switch, no real pain, just flick the switch.  However, I switched to
openwrt [0] and am enjoying it much better. [1]

[...]
> And ASUS put their own GPL code in the ring, notably for multiple SSID 
> support (the chip will support up to 16 SSIDs, though all of them will 
> be on the same channel).  Though I can't figure out how to VLAN them off 
> just yet because I want to use a second SSID for our new roomie and I 
> want to give her just Internet access while I give my own SSID access to 
> my entire core network.

I don't know if multiple SSID is really all that great a bag.  If you go with
openwrt, it has full iptables and you can do mac based filtering. [2]

--Robert

[0] http://openwrt.org
[1] Read: it stays up, has a web interface, I can add packages to it easily
and I don't have to do the saving hokey pokey to get stuff to stay on the
device.
[2] I don't know if dd-wrt does this or not.  However, filtering based on mac
is going to be similar to ssid filtering but with slightly greater overhead.
I'm not sure if there's an iptables module for ssid filtering either.
