No.  I don't see this as a security risk.  There is no way to demonize google
(or anyone else that does this type of caching) because the information is put
on a public forum.  This is akin to giving out sensitive information live on an
obscure cable station and then blaming the broadcaster for airing your
information to its audience.  The things that go into the cloud need to be
checked for content before publishing if you believe there is *any* chance of
security being compromised.

On the other side of the coin, with all of the stupidity that is U.S. patent
and copyright law, the fact that google is caching the information makes prior
art searches easier.

My 2 cents.
