Thankfully System can be backup in 10 commands or less. thanks to a good
backup.

boot gentoo CD
mount new harddrive
copy backup
chroot enviornment
untar backup
re-grub the harddrive
exit chroot
unmount harddrive
reboot
Thats it.

See here is a PERFECT example of being a Quasi bad naught sys admin.  I have
a server (my own) running on a hard drive I KNOW is running on several years
of borrowed time.  I have replacement sitting on desk, and I keep telling
myself.  "You know you can wait another week to replace that hard drive.
It's not THAT important right now".  On the flip side, I badger my clients
into replacing anything IMMEDIATELY if I have lost faith in the hardware.
Everyday I preach two things,  Preventative maintenance, and backups.  I
have to say at least I listened to myself on the backups.

To all users out there, New, old, seasoned, etc.   BACK UP YOUR
DATA!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!! BACK IT UP. BACK IT UP. BACK IT UP.

Now you all might be saying. WOW this guy knows his stuff, he was prepared
he know how to quickly recover from data loss with minimal problems.  Well
I'm sad to say that THAT was a lesson I had to learn the difficult way too.
Fortunately it stuck the first time. SO I pass on some advice to thoes who
have not experience that gut sinking, bowel wrenching, teeth grinding, eye
budding and pant wetting experience of data/system/server loss.
