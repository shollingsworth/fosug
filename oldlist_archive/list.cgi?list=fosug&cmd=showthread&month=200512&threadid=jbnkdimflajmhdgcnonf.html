Echoing George's sentiments about not being a sysadmin, and since I
don't run a mail relay on my home network, I don't have a lot to add
to that part of the discussion.

On the other hand, I've found a great way to cut down on the spam my
personal account receives.  Since I own my own domain with unlimited
email forwarding, I just use a different (though logical to me) email
address with each entity I interact with online.  If any of those
email addresses get out in the wild, I can filter that email to
/dev/null, or anywhere else.  I can also decide at that point whether
I want to continue interacting with some entity that would be so
careless as to share my email address with spamers.

Only my close friends/family get my private email address.  It has
worked really well for several years.
