Although I do not know of anyone implementing such a creature, it would make
sense that you could implement an outbound bayesian filter much the same as
your inbound filter.

Ideally, you train this on all mail leaving your domain before you apply any
other rate-limiting techniques, recipient counts, etc.  I don't think you can
blatantly block using this, but you get a feel for what your user's email
looks like to the bayesian engine and spam flagged emails are either, 1) a new
customer who writes like absolutely nobody else or 2) a spam email.

Unfortunately, you would have to be _very_ liberal in your abuse policy and
use this more as a flagging technology than anything else.

One of the most novel ideas that I've seen to date is that those who have 25
blocking and an open relay mail server setup (for their ip space only, of
course) is that they take the connection information for each SMTP transaction
and embed it in the headers of the mail.  This lets them backtrace the mail
vital information to a SP controlled time/date stamp and an IP address.  At
least this eases the abuse complaint handling process.
