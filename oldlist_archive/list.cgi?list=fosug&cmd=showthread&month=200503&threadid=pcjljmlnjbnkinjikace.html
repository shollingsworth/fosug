There are several vendors out there with inexpensive stuff, like Voxilla 
where I got my Sipura, and Digit Networks (http://www.digitnetworks.com) 
which carries an equivalent of Digium's X100P FXO card for about $30-ish 
or so.

An FXO port is my next purchase so I can integrate my normal phone line. 
  Just haven't decided whether it'll be a card for the box or an 
ethernet-attached unit.  I just wish Digium's stuff didn't cost so much. 
  And for the time being, I'm a bigger fan of buying new and from 
reputable dealers (unless those eBay-bound Digiums are in fact new; then 
it's a question of dealer reputation).  Then I'm going to cut back even 
more on my telephone services on my normal line, letting either 
BroadVoice and/or Asterisk handle the rest.

Also, since I have two Internet connections, I'm considering moving the 
Asterisk behind my cable box and run it on NAT, then put up a SIP proxy 
on one of my servers behind my DSL connection (which is not NATted) so I 
can run IP phones on the outside to the Asterisk box.
