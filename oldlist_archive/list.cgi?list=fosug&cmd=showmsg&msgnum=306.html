My section governing the keyboard settings is as follows:

Section "InputDevice"
  Driver       "kbd"
  Identifier   "Keyboard[0]"
  Option       "Protocol" "Standard"
  Option       "XkbKeyCodes" "xfree86"
  Option       "XkbLayout" "us"
  Option       "XkbModel" "pc104"
EndSection

If yours is the same, I would look for another copy of the us.map.gz file and
dump it in place.

My us.map.gz is in /usr/X11R6/share/keymaps/i386/qwerty and has a md5sum of
34fc02ef080757cdc93e15b9302edab1 (if that helps at all).  I'd check to make
sure it's not a symbolic link.

I'd dig around in the included files and just do a search on quote.

Lastly, a fun vim tip is that you can hover over any single character and push
ga to get the ascii value in decimal, hex and octal.

e.g. Hover over the quote " and pushing ga gives:

<">  34,  Hex 22,  Octal 042

at the bottom of my vim screen.
