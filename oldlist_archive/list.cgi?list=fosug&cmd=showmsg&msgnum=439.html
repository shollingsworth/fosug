On 6/8/05, Joel Searcy ####@####.#### wrote:
> Any of you guys every use linux based thin clients?
> We will be setting them up at work.  We have a lot of
> budget and bandwidth constraints.  We will be using
> TFTP to download the kernel with bootscript and load
> it's own DKE desktop because of this.
> 
> I'm not one of the admins (I hope to get promodted to
> the NOC at some point in the future).  But can you
> think of a better way to do this.  With all of the
> clients running like that it may take a while to get
> new stations up and running, with more than 20 clients
> up it can take 2-5 minutes to download the kernel on a
> newly booting station.
> 
> Can anybody think of a better and/or easier way,
> possably a less bandwidth intensive way to do this?
> Processor power is not a problem.
> 

A.) Check where your bandwidth clogs are and possibly think about
subneting to relieve some of the stress.

B.) How often are you going to have all the workstations bounce at once?

or

On your TS server you may think about installing a gigabit card, and
maybe a gigabit hub as well. It's all about discovering where you
bottleneck is.

That's all I can think of.
