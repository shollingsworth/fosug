I've heard of cases of SBC blocking VPN traffic if you have over so much VPN traffic on residential accounts so hopefully that isn't the case.  
   
  Take in mind many other telco's outside of the US are buying software that analizes packets and can be configured to drop VPN, VoiP as well as P2P and torrent packets as well as other packet types.  The best examples I can think of for that are France and China telcos use it because of the monopolies there.  I heard SBC was considering purchasing it but I don't know if they ever did or implemented it.  They may 
   
  We are using the SonicWall client of course and the VPN connection isn't dropping off completely just slowing down intermitently and we only have 5 or less users connected at once through VPN most times.  Many of the applications we use require a constant connection and if it begins to drop in speed or the packets suddenly start to drop off, the connection to the application will drop as well which is where the problems occur.  Only our remote Verizon DSL employees are experiencing this, every body else has some way of connecting without issues.  This is by far the biggest issue with the cooperate IM that we use (Live Communication Server) and other apps as well.
   
  AOL is bad we all know that, it hijacks your system in many ways and they shut off ports when connecting through them, I'm not supprised you're having issues, the fix is don't go with AOL.  AOL = you get pwned!
   
  I'm not sure of the product version you have but ours came configured by default to use VPN over IPSec.  Make sure the clients are pointing to the IP and not a DNS pointer of some kind, change your default port and update the SonicWall VPN clients with the appropriate information and see if that helps Keith.  If yours is limited in functionality for some reason you may want to consider purchasing the new firmware upgrade they have available to give you more features.
   
  I haven't had any issues with SonicWall not working the way it's suppose to and everyone else can use it ok.
