Hi guys,

I know this is going to be a silly thread but I had an epiphany
http://dictionary.reference.com/search?q=ephiphany (see Reference 3a)

Anyhow, I decided to really learn about syslog-ng. So I read the
manual. We all read manuals right? But do we really read them, study
them and follow along by messing with our own machines?

I haven't been like that, and just by spending a few hours really
diving into a particular subject like using syslog-ng
(http://www.balabit.com/products/syslog_ng/reference/book1.html) I can
increase my awareness of what is going on with my machine, and even
give me real time alerts if something b0rk3d bad.

I know it's trivial to some, I just thought I'd share my thoughts with
the group. I've always been a trial by fire guy, and not so prone to
reading the manuals, but just doing it. I'm starting to realize,
especially in a production environment that we/I would be better
served by taking the time to learn it right the first time instead of
banging my head on the desk over the course of a few weeks or even
months.

Just some random insights by yours truly... thoughts anybody?

Cheers,

Stevo
