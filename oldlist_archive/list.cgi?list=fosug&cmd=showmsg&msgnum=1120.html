Drew,

I've been running SuSE 10.0 x86_64 for a couple of months with success,
including with my older bttv WinTV GO.  All of that even worked 64 bit with
with Ubuntu Dapper Drake beta.  The ivtv pvr I just installed and works with
neither but I have placed my diagnostic effort with SuSE KDE because I'm
more familiar with it.  SuSE appears to run both 32 and 64 bit apps with the
x86_64 kernel.  Part of the distribution is 32 bit and there are two sets of
libraries -- /lib and /lib64.  I find much of what I've read on the web
about 64 bit to be inconsistent and frequently not agreeing with what I see
working.  To be honest I'm a bit iffy on 64 bit myself.  They have done
something to facilitate mixing 32 and 64 but chroot hasn't been mentioned.
I know I can run a 32 bit kernel but as an old development engineer it seems
a little sacrilegious and a waist of the new horsepower I purchased for a
kick in performance.  My use of Linux is both production and learning.  I
prefer not to take the easy way out regardless of how sensible that approach
may be.

George
