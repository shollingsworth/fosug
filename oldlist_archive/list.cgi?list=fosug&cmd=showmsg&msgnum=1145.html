Hi Guys,
I would like to invite you all to attend the next meeting of the Central 
California .Net User Group:

Wednesday, May/10 at 6:00pm
Fresno State's University Business Center room 194
For a detailed map: http://centralcaldotnet.com/UBCMap.JPG
For more information visit: www.centralcaldotnet.com

It is 100% free for you and as many fellow developers as you’d like to 
bring, including free parking, food and soft drinks. Of course, as usual, 
we’ll have thousand of dollars worth of prizes to give away, including books 
and software titles.

The guest speaker is TIM HUCKABY and he'll be talking about WF (Windows 
Workflow Foundation), smart client development, and other cool and useful 
topics.

Thanks and I hope to see many of you there!

- Gustavo

About Tim Huckaby:
Tim was awarded for the highest rated Keynote presentations of all the 
Developer Days held around the world in 1998 and in 1999, took 3rd in 2000 
and top 10 in 2002 by Microsoft Corporation. Tim has done presentations on 
Microsoft Technologies at technology events like Microsoft Tech Ed, Product 
Launches events, Dev Days, MEC, World Wide Partner Conference, MGB, and the 
PDC, along with 3rd party technology conferences all over the world is 
consistently rated in the top 10% of all speakers at the events. Tim was 
selected by Microsoft as a “Rock-Star” speaker for the International .NET 
Association and speaks world-wide on their behalf.

Tim has over 20 years of industry experience. Besides being the CEO, Lead 
Technical Architect and Software Development Lead of Interknowlogy 
(www.interknowlogy.com), he is also:
•	Microsoft Regional Director (for San Diego, CA)
•	Renowned author
•	Microsoft Partner Advisory Council member
•	Microsoft e-Business Partner Advisory Council member
•	Microsoft MVP
•	International .NET Association speaker

Some of Tim’s clients: Microsoft, Pacific Life Insurance Company, Nabisco, 
Qualcomm, Gateway, Standard & Poors, Colonial Insurance Company, Mobile 
Planet, SkyMall, Kingston Technology, Cooking.com, Buy.com, the U.S. Navy 
and many others.

Tim is one of the few technologists who are not a Microsoft employee that 
has worked on a software product on a product team at Microsoft in Redmond, 
Washington. In his role as developer lead, he commuted to Redmond for six 
months to work in the architecture group on Microsoft Site Server Version 
3.0.
