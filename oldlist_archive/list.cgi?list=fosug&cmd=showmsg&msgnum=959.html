*nod*  There are some difficulties with linux and wifi vendors.  Why this is,
I'm reluctant to speculate about.  However, that said, I have a Cisco Aironet
350.  Great card.  Support under linux is great.  Best thing of all with the
350 is that it scans all available channels simultaneously when in promiscuous
mode.  That's right boys and girls, no channel hopping.

As for the atheros chips that were mentioned, I also have a proxim external
antenna model.  If you're looking for something strictly as a client, it's
great.  If you want to sniff your lan for troubleshooting, skip it.  I
couldn't even get the card I have to switch to promiscuous mode.

> For an AP, I'm using a Belkin F5D7230-4 v5000 because that was all I could
> afford until I get a suitable LinkSys WRT54G(S) so I can put Linux on it.

Can't help too much here.  I have a WRT54G at home (beware model 5, can't get
linux on it - see http://www.wrt54g.net) and a Aironet 1200 at work.

> Any ideas/thoughts/whatever?

Look to the source young man. [0]  Most of the well supported wireless devices
are listed there.  Once you find a card that meets the specs you want, buy it
online and save yourself the grief of the local hardware vendors.

If you're source averse (but I don't get that impression), you can look
through the kernel build menu under net/wireless and see what the comments
there have to say about support.

HTH,
--Robert
