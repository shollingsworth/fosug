On Tue, Nov 01, 2005 at 03:49:31PM -0800, Dennis Aston wrote:
> Hey, there was a thread a while back on this mailing list about sbc blocking
> port 25 on dynamic dsl accounts. Well, I just wanted to post a follow up to
> that. At the bottom of this page here...
>  http://support.sbcglobal.net/article.php?item=4640
>  There is a note that says this:
>  -------START CUT--------
>  To request removal of the filter, use the Abuse of Service form at
> http://help.sbcglobal.net/servabuse.php to contact our Abuse team and choose
> the "Opt-Out Port 25" under *Abuse Type*. Enter your account Member ID (also
> called your SBCIS PPPOE Login ID or User ID) to opt-out. If you have
> multiple SBCIS accounts you want to opt-out, please submit a separate form
> for each Member ID. *Do not* enter your email addresses from the other mail
> hosting provider. You should expect that it will take SBCIS 12-24 hours to
> process your request.
> 
> After receiving a confirmation email, sent to the SBCIS Member account ID
> being opted-out, you will need to logoff the SBCIS network and turn off your
> DSL router or modem for 1 minute to remove the filter. You should then turn
> your modem and system back on and log in using the normal procedure
>  -------END CUT-------
>  So, you should be able to remove port 25 filtering period. I think all of
> you are pretty good about getting around such things, but I wanted to put
> this up on this thread in case somebody else wanted to know this. I am
> putting this into play on my dynamic dsl clients.

I was the one who posted that. I just set Postfix to relay through SBC's
servers (that in and of itself was a PITA, as I'm an old PacBell.net account
and my mail relay (and setup for mail, etc. is diff. that the support pages
list.)

A lot of ISPs won't allow incoming mail from dynamic IPs as well. So it's best
to relay through the ISP.
