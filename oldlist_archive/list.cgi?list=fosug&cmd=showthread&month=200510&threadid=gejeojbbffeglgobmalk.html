Stevo wrote:

> Can someone please explain the free "as in beer" vs. the free "as in
> speech" thing.
> 
> I've heard this thrown around way too long to be ignorant of it's meaning.
> 
> And yes, I'm too lazy to look it up. Besides, I want to start a discussion.
> 
> ~stevo

"Free as in beer" means software you can obtain and use without paying
any manner of fee.  Source may or may not be included.

"Free as in speech" means you get the source code, regardless of how you
obtained it.  The GPL stands for, at the minimum, the "free as in
speech" model.

As it happens, most GPLed software falls under both headings.
