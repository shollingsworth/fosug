On the server side, I am very uncomfortable with its security history. 
If I have to assign someone access to the machine even if for a specific 
task, I'd feel far more comfortable if it were someone I could trust 
with full root access.  Granted, there are many other setuid programs 
they could exploit, but this one, by far, has me the most alarmed.

Put simply, sudo strikes me as a potential security nightmare when it 
comes to code audits.  su, on the other hand, is far simpler.  At that 
point, the onus of delegating privileges on the system rests squarely on 
the shoulders of the admin, where it truly belongs.  I can understand 
why some people would use it.  I personally am not one of those people. 
  To me, when it comes to privlege access at the command prompt level, 
it's all or nothing.

On the desktop side, I suppose I could live with it, but that still 
doesn't change my feelings overall toward sudo.
