Mike,

In addition to bringing everything up to date including things I hadn't
found to update on 9.3, it has a number of things that you might find
interesting. For one it includes the latest Openoffice and Mozilla aps
making it a good choice for a newbie. Many distros are released with older
versions of these aps. It has Novell software in the boxed release --
apparmor, ifolder 3. It has mono which gives you MS .NET and a couple of
VOIP aps like linphone. Bluetooth is now automatically recognized and boots
/ shutdowns are faster than 9.3. It has beagle desktop, directed at gnome --
like the google desktop. I know your a VMware user but xen is also included.
Xen isn't to the VMware level of features and release but its open source. I
always buy the boxed releases to support Novell with Linux but Novel has a
free GPL release that's already available for download at
www.opensuse.org<http://www.opensuse.org>.
This is a change -- in the past, free downloads came 3 months after the
boxed release. Now its virtually the same day. In 10.0 MP3 is directly
supported but you still have to download an uncrippled version of xine and
the dvdcss encryption to view commercial movies -- not a big deal. German
law is very strong in this area. I tried an update rather than the customary
clean install and it worked well with very little bit diddling -- its Linux
after all and we like it that way.

I have retail DVD and CDs. I think its worthwhile but it may be the geek and
not the practical person speaking.

Have a nice day,
George
